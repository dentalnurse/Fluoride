CPD comes up every year at renewal time and yet it still causes 
confusion for a lot of dental nurses. How many hours do you need? 
What counts as verifiable? What topics do you have to cover? And 
what actually happens if you do not keep up?

This post covers everything you need to know, taken directly from 
GDC guidance.

## What Is CPD?

The GDC defines CPD in law as "learning, training or other 
developmental activities which can reasonably be expected to 
maintain and develop a person's practice as a dentist or dental 
care professional, and is relevant to the person's field of 
practice."

In plain terms it is any activity that keeps your knowledge and 
skills up to date and is relevant to the work you actually do. 
The GDC requires it as a condition of registration because keeping 
up to date is how you continue to provide safe, appropriate care 
to your patients.

## How Many Hours Do Dental Nurses Need?

Dental nurses must complete a minimum of 50 hours of verifiable 
CPD in each five year cycle.

This is lower than some other registrant groups. For comparison, 
dentists need 100 hours and dental hygienists, therapists and 
orthodontic therapists need 75 hours each.

The GDC is clear that the minimum is not designed to drive your 
planning. You should plan activity around your actual learning 
and maintenance needs, and for many dental nurses this will go 
beyond the minimum.

## What Is Verifiable CPD?

All hours you submit to the GDC must be verifiable. The GDC 
stopped accepting non-verifiable CPD declarations, so everything 
you declare needs to meet the verifiable criteria.

To count as verifiable, CPD must:

- Have clear educational aims and objectives
- Have clear anticipated outcomes
- Be quality assured by the provider
- Provide documentary evidence of completion, such as a 
  certificate

The evidence you collect must match the hours you declare in 
your annual statement. If the GDC selects you for random 
sampling, you will need to produce this evidence, so keeping 
records carefully throughout the cycle matters.

## How Should Hours Be Spread Across the Cycle?

CPD is more beneficial when spread throughout the cycle rather 
than completed in a rush at the end. The GDC requires you to do 
at least 10 hours of CPD for every two consecutive CPD years. 
You must not declare two consecutive years of zero CPD.

You can have more than one zero hours year in a five year cycle 
as long as you meet the 10 hours every two years requirement.

The CPD year for dental care professionals runs from 1 August 
to 31 July each year.

## What Topics Does the GDC Recommend?

The GDC recommends certain CPD topics that it considers relevant 
to most dental professionals. These are recommendations, not 
mandatory requirements, but regularly refreshing knowledge in 
these areas contributes to patient safety and professional 
performance.

The recommended topics and suggested hours are:

- **Medical emergencies** -- at least 10 hours in each CPD cycle, 
  with at least 2 hours of this type of activity every year
- **Disinfection and decontamination** -- at least 5 hours in 
  each cycle
- **Radiography and radiation protection** -- at least 5 hours 
  in each cycle, if you undertake radiography

These are not mandatory minimums. They are the GDC's way of 
flagging topics it considers especially important for patient 
safety across the dental team.

## What Records Do You Need to Keep?

A complete CPD record for the GDC must include:

1. **A personal development plan (PDP)** -- created at the start 
   of your cycle, identifying your learning needs, the CPD you 
   plan to undertake, the development outcomes it links to, and 
   the timeframes you expect to complete it in. You should review 
   this annually.

2. **A log of completed activity** -- recording the title and 
   description of each activity, the date it was undertaken, 
   and the number of hours as shown on your evidence.

3. **Documentary evidence** -- a certificate or mapping document 
   from the CPD provider for every activity you declare.

You must keep these records for five years after your CPD cycle 
ends. The GDC may ask to see them as part of random sampling 
or during a fitness to practise investigation.

## The Development Outcomes

Every CPD activity you record must link to at least one of the 
GDC's development outcomes. These outcomes are:

- **A** -- Effective communication with patients, the dental 
  team and others across dentistry
- **B** -- Effective management of self and effective management 
  of others or effective work with others in the dental team, 
  in the interests of patients
- **C** -- Maintenance and development of knowledge and skill 
  within your field of practice
- **D** -- Maintenance of skills, behaviours and attitudes which 
  maintain patient confidence in you and the dental profession 
  and put patients' interests first

When you choose a CPD activity, think about which of these 
outcomes it maps to. This is also what CPD providers should 
be showing on their certificates.

## Annual and End of Cycle Statements

Each year you must make an annual CPD statement through your 
eGDC account. You are declaring:

- The number of CPD hours you have completed that year
- That you have kept a CPD record
- That the activity you have done is relevant to your practice

You can update your statement at any point in the year. At the 
end of your five year cycle you must also submit an end of cycle 
statement confirming you have met the requirements.

If you do not make your annual statement or meet the requirements, 
you may be putting your registration at risk.

## What If You Cannot Complete Your Hours in Time?

If you are in the final year of your cycle and have exceptional 
circumstances that have prevented you from completing your hours, 
you can apply to the GDC for a grace period. This can only be 
requested in the last six months of your cycle and gives you an 
additional 56 days to complete your CPD. It is not automatic and 
requires an application with an explanation.
