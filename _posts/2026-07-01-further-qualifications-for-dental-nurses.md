Completing a fluoride varnish qualification is a great starting point. 
It is often the first time a dental nurse steps outside their core 
registration and takes on an extended duty independently. And once you 
have done it, it is hard not to start thinking about what comes next.

The good news is there is plenty to aim for.

Dental Nurse Training has been delivering regulated dental nursing 
qualifications since 1995. Alongside the Certificate in Fluoride 
Varnish Application, there is a full range of post-registration 
qualifications available for dental nurses who want to keep developing.

Before taking on any extended duty, make sure you have the right 
training, competence and indemnity in place. Read our post on [GDC 
scope of practice for dental nurses](https://dentalnurse.github.io/Fluoride/blog/can-dental-nurses-apply-fluoride-varnish-gdc-scope-of-practice-2025/) for a full explanation of what is required.

## Not Yet Qualified?

If you have landed here and you are not yet a registered dental nurse, 
the first step is the NEBDN Level 3 Diploma in Dental Nursing. This 
is the qualification that leads to GDC registration, and without GDC 
registration you cannot apply fluoride varnish or any other extended 
duty independently.

The NEBDN diploma is regulated by Ofqual and CCEA and also carries 
32 UCAS points, which can open doors to further education if you want 
to go that route later on.

[Find out more about the NEBDN Diploma at Dental Nurse Training.](https://dentalnurse.training/product/diploma-in-dental-nursing/)

## Dental Radiography

Radiography is one of the most popular post-registration qualifications 
for dental nurses, and it is easy to see why. Being able to take 
radiographs under prescription adds real value to any dental team and 
opens up a wider range of clinical responsibilities.

There are two routes available:

**NEBDN Certificate in Dental Radiography** -- on qualification you 
will be able to act as an IR(ME)R operator under prescription, taking 
intra-oral and extra-oral radiographs safely and independently.

**BDA Certificate in Dental Radiography** -- a nationally recognised 
qualification approved by the GDC, the British Society of Dental and 
Maxillofacial Radiology, and the Society and College of Radiographers.

[Find out more about radiography qualifications at Dental Nurse 
Training.](https://dentalnurse.training)

## Oral Health Education

Oral health education is another great route for dental nurses who 
enjoy the patient-facing, preventive side of dentistry. It builds 
directly on the kind of work you are already doing when you apply 
fluoride varnish and give aftercare advice, but takes it much further.

Again there are two routes:

**NEBDN Certificate in Oral Health Education** -- a valuable 
post-registration qualification for dental nurses hoping to further 
their careers in preventive care and patient education.

**BDA Certificate in Oral Health Education** -- a nationally 
recognised qualification designed to equip dental nurses with the 
knowledge and skills to deliver high-quality oral health advice and 
support, approved by the GDC and created in collaboration with 
OHE Southwest.

[Find out more about oral health education qualifications at Dental 
Nurse Training.](https://dentalnurse.training)

## Which Should I Do Next?

There is no single right answer. It depends on what you enjoy 
clinically, what your practice needs, and where you want to take 
your career.

Radiography tends to suit dental nurses who enjoy the technical 
side of dentistry and want to take on a more clinical role within 
the surgery. Oral health education suits those who enjoy spending 
more time with patients, working on behaviour change and prevention, 
or who are interested in community or public health settings.

Both are well regarded by employers and both count towards your 
GDC CPD requirements. For a full guide to CPD, read our post on 
[CPD requirements for dental nurses](https://dentalnurse.github.io/Fluoride/blog/cpd-requirements-for-dental-nurses/).
