Portfolio of Evidence

What your portfolio must contain and how it is structured.

Part 1 — Witnessed Application Logs

You must complete a minimum of 10 supervised fluoride varnish applications, each directly observed and signed off by your GDC-registered clinical supervisor on the day of the application. Retrospective sign-off is not permitted.

  • At least 3 of the 10 applications must be on adult patients
  • Applications must span all five required patient groups
  • Each log includes a 10-point competency checklist, supervisor comments and signature, and your reflective log entry
  • Your supervisor must have signed the Witness Declaration before any applications begin

Part 2 — Written Tasks

Three written tasks demonstrating your understanding of the underpinning theory. Each should be 400–600 words and draw on your own clinical experience and learning from the three modules.

T1

Explain the consent process as it applies to fluoride varnish application

400–600 words. Cover valid consent, patient groups, documentation, and what to do if a patient declines.

T2

Explain your responsibilities as a GDC registrant undertaking extended duties

400–600 words. Cover scope of practice, maintaining competence, raising concerns, record keeping and indemnity.

T3

Explain the importance of cross infection control in fluoride varnish application

400–600 words. Cover standard precautions, PPE, hand hygiene, decontamination and clinical environment.

Part 3 — Extended Case Study

Choose one of your 10 witnessed applications and expand it into a full case study of 1,000–1,500 words. This must be a real clinical case with all patient details anonymised in line with GDPR.

Your case study must cover:

  • Patient background and relevant medical/dental history (anonymised)
  • Caries risk assessment and rationale for fluoride varnish application
  • The full treatment plan of which fluoride application forms a part — not just the application in isolation
  • The fluoride varnish application itself — product, technique, any challenges
  • Communication, aftercare and follow-up
  • Personal reflection — what you learned and what you would do differently

Reflective Log

A reflection of 200–300 words is required after each of the 10 witnessed applications. Reflections should address: what went well, what was challenging, and what you will do differently next time. These should be genuine and specific — not repeated identically across applications.

Submitting your portfolio

Submit your completed portfolio to DNT via dentalnurse.training. Your tutor will review and respond within 10 working days. If any part requires resubmission, written feedback will be provided with a maximum 28-day resubmission window.
