Course Overview

Everything you need to know about the DNT Certificate in Fluoride Varnish Application.

About this qualification

The DNT Certificate in Fluoride Varnish Application is a GDC-compliant extended duties qualification for registered dental nurses. It meets the GDC Scope of Practice requirements (November 2025) for dental nurses to apply fluoride varnish — namely that the learner is appropriately trained, demonstrably competent, and indemnified.

The course is delivered by Em Bremner of DNT — Dental Nurse Training. On successful completion you will receive a named DNT certificate supporting your personal scope of practice as a GDC registrant.

2026 NHS contract reform

The NHS (Primary Dental Services) Amendment Regulations 2026, in force from 1 April 2026, formally recognise standalone fluoride varnish delivered by a dental nurse on a dentist's prescription as an NHS course of treatment. A new 0.5 UDA course allows suitably skilled dental nurses to apply fluoride varnish without the patient requiring a full examination first.

How the course works

The course is blended: theory is delivered through online video and/or live/recorded lectures across three modules, and assessment is portfolio-based — combining supervised clinical applications, written tasks, and a case study.

There is no written examination. Assessment is entirely through your portfolio of evidence, which is reviewed by your DNT tutor.

The three modules

01

Legislation, Professionalism & Emergencies

GDPR, consent, Mental Capacity Act 2005, GDC standards, record keeping, CPR, anaphylaxis, fluoride toxicity.

→ 02

Health, Safety & Fluoride Materials

H&S legislation, COSHH, HTM 01-05, PPE, hand hygiene, decontamination, fluoride evidence base, patient groups.

→ 03

Clinical Application & Patient Care

Oral anatomy, application technique, communication, managing refusal, referral pathways, aftercare advice.

Assessment

Your portfolio of evidence must include:

  • 10 witnessed application logs — signed by your supervisor on the day. At least 3 on adult patients. Must cover all five patient groups.
  • Written Task 1 — Explain the consent process (400–600 words)
  • Written Task 2 — Explain your responsibilities as a GDC registrant (400–600 words)
  • Written Task 3 — Explain the importance of cross infection control (400–600 words)
  • Extended case study — 1,000–1,500 words, one of your 10 applications expanded into a full case including the complete treatment plan
  • Reflective log — 200–300 words after each of the 10 applications

Entry requirements

  • Current GDC registration as a dental nurse
  • Minimum 6 months post-registration clinical experience
  • Active clinical practice with access to patients across the required patient groups
  • Named GDC-registered clinical supervisor (dentist, hygienist or therapist)
  • Up-to-date CPD and active PDP
  • Appropriate professional indemnity insurance covering extended duties

Your clinical supervisor

Your supervisor must be GDC-registered, qualified and competent in fluoride varnish application, and willing to directly observe all 10 applications. They must read and sign the DNT Witness Declaration before any applications take place.

Important

Your supervisor must be physically present and directly observing during each application they sign off. Retrospective sign-off is not acceptable and constitutes falsification of professional records. The Witness Declaration makes clear to supervisors the professional consequences of signing off work they have not observed.
