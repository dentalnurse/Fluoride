1 Patient Aftercare Card

Print and hand to the patient (or parent/carer) immediately after fluoride varnish application.

🦷

Fluoride Varnish Aftercare

Dental Nurse Training Ltd — dentalnurse.training

Important instructions to follow today
  • ✓ Do not eat or drink for 30 minutes after treatment. This allows the varnish to absorb into the tooth surface.
  • ✓ Do not brush your teeth until tomorrow morning. Brushing too soon removes the varnish before it has had chance to work fully.
  • ✓ Do not rinse your mouth for the rest of the day. Rinsing washes away the fluoride.
  • ✓ Avoid hard, sticky or crunchy foods for the rest of the day (e.g. toffee, crusty bread, hard sweets). Soft foods and drinks are fine after 30 minutes.
  • ✓ A slight yellow tint on the teeth is completely normal and temporary. It will disappear when you brush tomorrow morning.
  • ✓ Continue your normal routine from tomorrow: brush twice daily with fluoride toothpaste, spit — do not rinse.
Any concerns? Contact your dental practice.

2 Logbook Entry Checklist

Every logbook entry must include all of the following to support GDC record-keeping standards. Tick each item off before submitting your logbook for supervisor sign-off.

Patient identifier (initials or case number — never full name in a portable logbook)
Age group or date of birth (e.g. child under 3 / adult)
Date of application
Prescription details: prescribing dentist's name & GDC number
Product name and concentration (e.g. Duraphat 22,600 ppm)
Surfaces treated (e.g. all surfaces / occlusal only / upper arch)
Safety screening completed: asthma, allergy to colophony, oral ulceration
Consent type: patient's own verbal consent OR parent/guardian consent with patient's name
Post-operative instructions given (verbal and/or written aftercare card)
Any adverse events or deferred surfaces (e.g. ulcer present UR1 area — deferred)
Supervisor name and GDC registration number
Supervisor signature confirming attendance and supervision

3 Quick Reference: DBOH Fluoride Varnish Recommendations

Based on Delivering Better Oral Health (4th edition). Always verify against the current edition.

Age Group Caries Risk Recommended Frequency Notes
Under 3 years High risk only 2× per year Apply only under supervision of a dentist; parental/carer consent required; use knee-to-knee technique for very young children
3 years and over All patients
(regardless of risk)		 2× per year Applies to all children aged 3+ irrespective of caries risk; high-risk patients may receive more frequent applications
3 years and over Moderate or high risk 2–4× per year Increased frequency for patients with active caries, high sugar intake, compromised saliva or previous restorations
Adults Moderate or high risk 2× per year Particularly important for patients with xerostomia, root surface exposure, orthodontic appliances or high caries experience
Older adults High risk (e.g. dry mouth, polypharmacy) 2–4× per year Root caries risk is high in this group; ensure safe seating position and manage xerostomia as a primary risk factor

DBOH = Delivering Better Oral Health (Public Health England / UKHSA, NHS England, Health Education England). Standard UK varnish concentration: 22,600 ppm (2.26% NaF). Varnish sets on contact with saliva.

4 Contraindications at a Glance

Check ALL of these before every application. If any are present, defer the relevant area or the full appointment and document.

  • Known allergy to colophony (a resin present in most fluoride varnishes) — full contraindication
  • Known allergy to any component of the specific varnish product being used
  • Active oral ulceration in the area to be treated — defer that area; treat unaffected areas
  • Stomatitis or painful oral mucosal lesions — full deferral until resolved
  • Acute asthma attack at time of appointment — defer until asthma is stable
  • Severe or brittle asthma not under control — seek prescriber guidance; some varnishes contain solvents
  • No valid prescription or PSD in place from a registered prescriber
  • Patient or guardian refusal — document and inform the supervising dentist
  • Expired or recalled product — do not use; dispose of as clinical waste
  • Gingival bleeding or significant gingival inflammation throughout the mouth — consider deferral and refer
Remember: Anxiety about the procedure, the presence of fillings, crowns or other restorations, and use of fluoride toothpaste at home are not contraindications. If in any doubt, seek advice from your supervising dentist before proceeding.
